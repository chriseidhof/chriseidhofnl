I've been thinking a lot about what my role (as a teacher) is with the LLMs. To do so, I also want to better understand what software development will look like a few years from now.

If the trend continues, it might become true that building software is essentially free. I think it's already true that experienced people can very quickly prompt themselves to a finished piece of software, and might become even quicker with an even higher quality.

You already see people building massive amounts of software with LLMs. Look at [Peter's list](https://github.com/steipete) of current project: at the time of writing it's 44 projects. Likewise, I read the following quote by Armin Ronacher:

> I ended up building and building and creating a ton of tools I did not end up using much. ([source](https://lucumr.pocoo.org/2026/1/18/agent-psychosis/))

Yesterday I chatted to [Alper](https://alper.nl) about this topic. He's often been on the right side of predicting the future. For example, he predicted [chat as an interface](https://alper.nl/posts/chat-as-an-important-new-platform-for-user-experience/) in 2015. One thing he mentioned which stuck: when it becomes practically free to build software, the bottleneck will be people who judge *what to build*.
