When Building Becomes Free

Please don’t share this, it’s not published yet.

I’ve been thinking a lot about what my role (as a teacher) is with the LLMs. To do so, I also want to better understand what software development will look like a few years from now.

If the trend continues, it might become true that building software is essentially free. I think it’s already true that experienced people can very quickly prompt themselves to a finished piece of software, and might become even quicker with an even higher quality.

You already see people building massive amounts of software with LLMs. Look at Peter’s list of current project: at the time of writing it’s 44 projects. Likewise, I read the following quote by Armin Ronacher:

I ended up building and building and creating a ton of tools I did not end up using much. (source)

Yesterday I chatted to Alper about this topic. He’s often been on the right side of predicting the future. For example, he predicted chat as an interface in 2015. One thing he mentioned which stuck: when it becomes practically free to build software, the bottleneck will be people who judge what to build .
